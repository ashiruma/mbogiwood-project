BEHIND THE LENS

How Wanjiru shot her first documentary with just a phone.

Published on June 26, 2025 by the Mbogiwood Team

In the bustling streets of Kibera, one filmmaker is proving that vision is more important than gear. We sat down with Wanjiru Kimani to discuss her groundbreaking documentary, 'Simu Story', and the power of mobile filmmaking. Her journey began not in a film school, but on the very streets she sought to document, armed with nothing more than her smartphone and an unshakeable belief in the stories of her community.

"The best camera is the one you have with you," Wanjiru says, a mantra she has not only adopted but proven. "I realized waiting for a RED camera was just an excuse. The stories were happening now, and my phone was powerful enough to capture their truth."

'Simu Story' follows the lives of three entrepreneurs in Kibera, each using mobile technology to build their businesses. The film is a raw, intimate, and ultimately hopeful look at innovation in one of Africa's largest informal settlements. The technical limitations of a phone, Wanjiru explains, became a creative advantage.

"You become invisible with a phone," she notes. "People are not intimidated. You can capture genuine moments that a large crew would scare away. It forced me to focus on the essentials: light, composition, and a human connection with my subjects."
